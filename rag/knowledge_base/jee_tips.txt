JEE MATH TIPS AND TRICKS

General Tips:
1. Always verify your answer by checking edge cases and boundary conditions.
2. For JEE problems, look for elegant solutions - brute force often leads to errors.
3. In probability, drawing a tree diagram clarifies complex conditional scenarios.
4. When solving inequalities, always test a value in each interval to confirm the sign.
5. For limits involving trig, substitute x=0 and check the form before applying L'Hopital.
6. In optimization, always check the second derivative to confirm max vs min.
7. For quadratics, if you need only the nature of roots, the discriminant is sufficient.
8. GP problems: identify r carefully - check if r = next term / current term is consistent.
9. Matrix determinant = 0 means linear dependence of rows/columns.
10. For binomial probability, identify n (trials), p (success probability), k (successes needed).

Time-saving Tricks:
11. For sum of squares: 1^2 + 2^2 + ... + n^2 = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
12. For sum of cubes: 1^3 + 2^3 + ... + n^3 = [n(n+1)/2]^2
13. Sum of first n natural numbers: n(n+1)/2
14. For integration by parts, use ILATE order: Inverse trig, Log, Algebraic, Trig, Exponential
15. Recognize standard forms quickly: difference of squares, perfect square trinomial, sum/difference of cubes.

Domain Constraints to Always Check:
16. Square root: argument must be >= 0
17. Logarithm: argument must be > 0, base must be > 0 and != 1
18. Division: denominator must be != 0
19. Probability: result must be between 0 and 1 inclusive
20. Physical quantities: lengths, areas, volumes must be positive
